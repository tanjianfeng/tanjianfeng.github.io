About me

I lead and build core infrastructure across operating systems, container runtimes, and AI systems, with a focus on cloud-native and AI-native platforms.

My work at Ant Group has centered on deterministic systems under real-world constraints — where performance, predictability, and operational correctness matter more than theoretical elegance. Through this work, I helped shape two major lines of system techniques, DeepSYS and DeepXPU, aimed at improving efficiency, isolation, and system-level control in large-scale production environments.

What follows is a structured view of the infrastructure ingredients I’ve built and evolved throughout my career.

DeepSYS

I built a cloud Linux for Ant Group for multi-vendor CPUs, improving performance, stability, isolation, and testing, and created NanoVisor (gVisor-based) as a secure container runtime for long-running containers, plus a FaaS runtime to reduce cold start time. I also apply a “research in production” methodology in DeepSYS: drawing insights from production and verifying solutions in production.

DeepXPU

DeepXPU is a full-stack optimization methodology that links software and hardware to raise utilization and performance as AI demand surges. It focuses on heterogeneous systems via software-and-hardware co-design, kernel optimizations, engine and framework work, heterogeneous virtualization, and close collaboration with algorithm teams for large-scale deployments.

References: Diffusion LLM and R-Fork.
